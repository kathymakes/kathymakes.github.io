The Hyphen Space 双重文化的世界

I was born in the hyphen space: a Chinese hyphen American kid. 我是个在双重文化世界里长大的中国美国人。 Growing up, I befriended those like me. 长大的朋友们，多多也与我类似。 Korean hyphen Americans, Indian hyphen Americans. 韩国美国人，印度美国人。 Kids who had to explain to their parents what certain words in newspapers meant. 这些孩子们常需帮助他们父母解释报纸上一些生涩词汇之意。 We lived dual lives, switching tongues when we walked through doors, and going to Chinese, Hindi, Korean, and Russian school on weekends. 我们过着双重的文化生活，进出家门常常需要切换两种语言，周末则去中文，印地文, 韩语或俄语学校。

I want to think of home broadly and abstractly. 我想从更广泛及抽象的意义上审视家的意义。 What is home ? 什么是家？ Home is familiar territory, it is habitual rhythms, the people who raised you. 家是熟悉的领地，是习惯的节奏，是养育你的人们。 For kids of immigrant families, home is often that private space created by a shared speech. 对于移民家庭的孩子们而言，家更代表一种共享的语言带来亲蜜感。 This language, like your home, is nothing that any random stranger could access. 这种特定的语言，就像你的家，不是随便什么陌生人都能步入的。

In the same way, all the life lessons from my family exist in either English or Mandarin, sometimes both at once. 对我而言，中文普通话是英语之外的主要用语。我的所有生活体验或中或英，有时两者兼之。 The second language creates another layer through which only other Mandarin and English speakers can enter. 我渐渐体会道，一个会二语的人，有一个只有其他懂同样二语的人才能共享的空间。

On Happy Living 快乐生活

Growing up, my parents drilled a hardcore work ethic into me. 我父母对我从小就树立了很强的劳动观念。 Their philosophy was, Work hard, play hard, and 他们的理念是，先勤奋工作，后娱乐放松，及 孩子长大吃点苦有好处，从小应该学着干活。 Kids should go through some hardship growing up. Like all immigrant parents, they dreamed big, hoping to see us in the crimson Harvard robes, winning Nobel Prizes, becoming the president of the United States. 像所有移民的父母一样，他们梦想期待着有一天我们会身着哈佛深红长袍，或赢得诺贝尔，要不成为美国的总统。

In the beginning, they were constantly hard-pressed for money, even though both were lucky to snag jobs as electrical engineers. 早年他们是穷学生，生活捉襟见肘。我父母都很幸运几年后都谋到电气工程师之职，但他们仍很节俭。 That was the 1990s. 那是上世纪九十年代。 When I was a kid, they'd always tell me how sparsely furnished their early home was—a table, a couple chairs, a mattress. 我小的时候，他们常说最早的家中只有一张桌子，两把椅子及一张床垫。 In the first decade of our lives, my brother and I were taught that work was life. 在我们的童年记忆中，我和弟弟只知道工作就是生活。

Later, in the security of their mid-life careers, my parents adjusted their ideas. 渐渐他们人到中年，事业有成，我父母的想法也开始变化。 These days, they often tell us to be less frugal, and to spend each day contently. 近年来，他们经常说不要太俭朴，可以怡然消费些。 Money isn't everything. 钱不是万能的。 该省就省，该花就花 Spend and save as needed is what my Dad says. 爸爸说。 My mom, she loves to laugh, and point out what a good life that I have. 我妈妈常笑说，我有多好的生活。 She's a fun, spontaneous person, and always tries to take everything positively. 妈妈是个即兴随意的人，看世事积极乐观。 She's a big fan of the phrase, Do what you love, love what you do. 她酷爱的对联是，做你所爱之事，爱你所做之事。 They raised my brother and me well, I think: happy, grateful, and down-to-earth. 我觉得父母对我和弟弟威利教育很到位：快乐，感恩和踏实。

On Health 健康

Food in Chinese culture is the glue of social interaction, and the center of family life. 在中国文化里饮食是社会交往的粘合剂，也是家庭生活的中心。 Whenever I come home, my grandma always asks me, 每当我回家的时候，奶奶问我， 孩子，你想吃儿点儿什么 ? Child, what are you craving ? These days, I reluctantly skip breakfast every morning; back at home, that'd be considered a veritable crime, almost as scold-worthy as poor academic performance. 这些天，我常常顾不上早餐。要是在家里这一定是不可饶恕的犯罪， 像会学习成绩不好一样被痛骂。

I'm not sure what the turning point was, but sometime during my high school years, my family became more concerned about the side effects of stress. 不知道何事的关系，大约在我的高中时代，他们开始注思想压力的副作用。 身体是第一, Your health is number one. Dad always emphasizes. 爸爸总是强调。 Now he brags about exercising all the time. 现在，他常以经常锻炼而自豪。 A decade before, our family members lived transfixed in front of screens, and the treadmill remained untouched for years. 十年前，周末里你会常常看到我们全家个坐在电脑屏幕前。

This year, after my grandma got seriously ill, we treated the topic of health with a kind of hyper-awareness. 今年自从我奶奶得了场重病，我们对待健康有了一种超常的意识。 The ordeal really shook up the whole family. 这事件震撼了整个家庭。 Grandma tells me all the time now, 她现在总是说 当心千万别生病。 Be careful to never get this sick. When sickness does befall someone, Chinese remedies like herbal medicines and massage are often prescribed before Western drugs. 得病时，除用西药外，我们也采用一些中医的疗法包括用草药和按摩。 Ginger pear soup, mmm, that's my favorite cold remedy. 姜梨汤，嗯，这是我最喜欢的感冒药。

On Friendship 友谊

I grew up shy, and often looked up to my mom on how to deal with people. 我从小害羞，遇见生人我常常抬头向妈妈寻救。 She was the opposite of me—loud in public, and never afraid to ask questions. 她与我相反，在公共场合大声说话，也从不害怕问问题。 Whenever I was being overly-sensitive, she'd say, Have thicker skin! 每当我不好意思，她会告诉我，脸皮厚点！

When I was dealing with trouble in friendships or within the family, she'd get real with me about it, and tell me, 当我与朋友和及家人有矛盾的时候，她都会好好和我谈心，开导我： 世界了什么样人都有。 There are all sorts of people in the world. Both Mom and Dad preached generosity among friends. At home, be frugal, but with friends, never skimp. 妈妈和爸爸都告诉我要对朋友慷慨：在家里要节省，但对朋友不吝啬。 Before events, she'd remind us, 每次出外做客，她都提醒我们， 千万别到别空手去人家。 Never go to anyone's place emptyhanded. She'd often send us kids on delivery jobs to friends' homes with edible gifts: home-grown produce like bokchoy and treats like clementines and Chinese bread. 她常让我们孩子跑腿送给朋友们一些可食用的礼物 比如自制农产像大白菜, 一盒柑橘,家做的 中国面点等等。

Growing up, my brother and I maintained a small circle. 长大过程中，我弟弟哥和我都只保持着一个小的社交圈。 We were content with just a couple close friends. 我们满足只有几个亲密的朋友。 We've always been very family-oriented, too, which I think stems from Chinese culture. 我们一直以家庭为中心，这可能是源于中国文化的本。 (Many Chinese-American families I know have three generations living under one roof.) (我认识的许多中国-美国家庭，都是三代同堂。) While my parents do nag us about networking, in the end they've taught us to value genuine relationships. 虽然父母常让我们要多对外交往，他们教我们最要懂得珍惜这些真诚的关系。 Quality over quantity: You only need a couple good friends to be truly content. 要质量不是数量：只要有几个要好的朋友，你就满足幸福。。 Oh and also, never let screentime replace facetime. 另外，屏幕的时间永远不能取代面对面的时间。

On Success 成功

My dad and paternal grandparents were more traditionally Chinese with their career and academic advice. 我爸爸和爷爷奶奶在职业和学业考虑上有较传统的中国观念。 Grandpa seldom spoke to us, but when he did, he'd say things like 我的爷爷话不多，但一旦开口，他便说 好好学习 ! Study hard ! Grandma often said, 奶奶对我们说， 好好听老师的话. Listen to everything your teachers say. In Asian culture, there's much more reverence for higher-ups, for better or for worse. 一般在亚洲文化里，不管上级是对不对，都要尊重他。

My mom's advice was a bit more Western. 我妈妈的观点更西化些。 She was the most comfortable with sending me to art school, and always stood behind my decision to pursue my passion. 她比较赞同我去艺术学院就读，并始终支持我追求我之所爱。 In her childhood stories, she was the attentive, excited student who asked tons of questions: 小时候，她是个用心好学又很还爱问问题的好学生。 别怕问问题，不好意思什么吗！ Just ask, what's there to be embarrassed about ? She also taught me to assert myself in the workplace. 她还教我在工作中要自信大胆。 She always bragged that she was never afraid to make mistakes speaking English, and that's why she's so comfortable talking today. 她骄傲的说，她不怕说英语犯错误，所以她今天英语能说得这么流利顺畅。 She has adapted so well to the Western workplace. Her fun spirit and extraversion really shine. 她十分适应西方工作方式，这与她外向的个性和乐观的态度一定有关。

Both of my parents were token success kids in China growing up, meaning they were bright students who played by the rules and aced tests. 在中国长大我父母是他们同龄的楷模，他们当时一定是尊守规则考试优秀的好学生。 In America, they learned that risk-taking matters more, and your ability to talk made a big difference. 今天在美国，他们懂得大胆勇为也更重要，而且要能说会道才行。 They used to teach us to play safe, but these days they urge us to be a little more cunning and courageous. 他们曾教我们做事稳妥，但现在则敦促我们要学精明和勇敢。 My dad, who found a stable job in a big corporation, tells us not to fear failure when we're still so young. 一直在大公司有份稳定工作的爸爸告诉我们：年轻人不要害怕失败。 Stability is not that interesting. 稳定不是那么有意思。 They also remind us to protect our own interests, and work smarter instead of harder. 他们也提醒我们要学会保护自己的利益，要聪明地干，而不只是苦干。

When I talk on the phone with my parents, I realized that I rarely ever notice the language shift. 但我和父母电话聊天，我很少意识到两种语言的切换。 We switch between the two without thinking, like amphibians moving between land and water. 这种语言的混用是下意识的，就像两栖动物在陆地和水里一样。 It’s a kind of hybrid language; you could call it Chinglish. 这种混合语言就叫中英文了。

While I consider English my primary tongue, I’ve become so atuned to both worlds, and feel a certain fluidity when translating mentally between the two. 虽然我自认英文为母语，但感觉我对这两个世界都很同语。似在两个精神世界里出入自由。 Growing up bilingual has helped me understand the world with more complexity and richness. 在双语的环境里长大，让我对这个世界复杂及丰富性有更好的了解。 Those nuances that get lost in translation all add an extra dimensionality. 有幸能理解那些很难翻译的细节及微妙之处，增加我对世界更多一维的认识。
