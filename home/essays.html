  • I was born in the hyphen space: a Chinese hyphen American kid. Growing up, I befriended those like me. Japanese hyphen Americans, Indian hyphen Americans.   • I want to think of home broadly and abstractly. What is home? Home is familiar territory, it is habitual rhythms, the people who raised you. For second-generation kids, home sometimes is language. There’s something lovely about the way you step through the door and into a private space created by a shared speech. This language, like your home, is nothing that any random stranger could access. 我想思故乡广泛和抽象。什么是家？家是熟悉的领域，这是习惯性的节奏，谁提出你的人。对于第二代的孩子，家里有时是语言。还有一些可爱的你穿过大门，进入一个共享的讲话创造了一个私密的空间一步的方式。这种语言，就像你的家，是什么，任何随机的陌生人可以访问。
